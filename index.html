About Climate Change

Climate change refers to significant changes in global temperatures and weather patterns over time. While climate change is a natural phenomenon, scientific evidence shows that human activities are currently driving an unprecedented rate of change.

Causes of Climate Change

Human activities, especially the burning of fossil fuels and deforestation, have increased greenhouse gas levels in the atmosphere, which trap heat and cause the planet to warm.

Effects of Climate Change

Climate change is causing more frequent and severe weather events (including longer droughts, wildfires, stronger hurricanes, and extreme rainfall), rising sea levels, and disruptions to ecosystems and agriculture. Climate change has been increasing drastically over the years, which is a huge concern.

Solutions to Climate Change

To mitigate climate change, we need to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, transition to renewable energy sources, and adopt sustainable practices in all aspects of life. In short terms, becoming eco-friendly is the only way we can tackle climate change at this point.
